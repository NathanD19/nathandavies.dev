Smart Parking for Smart Cities

Background

Searching for a parking space has become a common negative experience for many people in many cities; particularly ones with high population density. This leads to wasting time, money, fuel and increasing impacts such as pollution and congestion which leads to dissatisfaction for the community.

Smart allocation of parking spaces is one of the steps towards smart cities, removes the economic and environmental implications and brings satisfaction for the community. This project has been initiated by Zeinab Farahmandpour; A PhD student of Swinburne who has a strong passion for artificial intelligence and the Internet of Things (IoT).

Roles

  • Research: Participated in and combined research materials on subject matter.
  • Android Usability: Establishing criteria for a suitable user experience.
  • UX Design and Prototyping: Design and build prototypes of the android and web user interfaces.
  • Android Development: Developer on Native Android Mobile App

Technical Components

  • Android Application to handle user input and provide suitable output and data.
  • Node.JS Server Used to process and manage incoming requests from the Android application.
  • Hardware Prototype used to test and demonstrate the capabilites of the software.

Projects Conclusion

The team has currently completed a system in which users can view available spaces and Users can book a space for a specific time. Once a user has parked in their space, the system will mark the space as occupied and authenticate that the correct car is parked in the space. We have begun laying the groundwork for the next stage. Real-world hardware already has an interface to the system when this is implemented.
